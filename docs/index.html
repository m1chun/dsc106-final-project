What Are Seed Zones?

California is divided into 85 seed zones based on long-term climate, elevation, and terrain. These zones are determined by the plant species that are naturally adapted to survive in each region.

Seed zones are critical for wildfire recovery because local vegetation has adapted to be more fire-resistant and regenerates more successfully. after burns.

California's Major Physiographic & Climatic Regions

To better understand wildfire behavior, California can be divided into nine broad physiographic and climatic regions. Each region has distinct vegetation, moisture, temperature, and wind patterns that strongly shapes fire risk.

Why Should You Care?

  1. Wildfires affect air quality statewide

    Even if you don’t live near fires, the smoke often travels hundreds of miles.

    Poor air quality impacts everyone’s health—kids, elderly people, people with asthma, athletes, and anyone who just wants to go outside.

  2. Fires threaten the places people love

    Parks, forests, hiking areas, and landmarks burn.

    Even if someone isn’t into “wildfire science,” they care about nature, recreation, and their favorite places.

  3. Wildfires affect climate—and climate affects everyone

    Fires release huge amounts of carbon.

    Bigger fires make climate change worse, and climate change increases fire risk in a feedback loop.
