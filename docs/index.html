What Are Seed Zones?

California is divided into 85 seed zones based on long-term climate, elevation, and terrain. These zones determine which plant species are naturally adapted to survive in each region.

Seed zones are critical for wildfire recovery because vegetation that is locally adapted is more fire-resistant and regenerates more successfully after burns.

California’s Major Physiographic & Climatic Regions

To better understand wildfire behavior, California can be divided into six broad physiographic and climatic regions. Each region has distinct vegetation, moisture, temperature, and wind patterns that strongly shape fire risk.
