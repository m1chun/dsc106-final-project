Project Overview

We like the idea of modeling fires like we did in Project 3, but wanted to work with different data more relevant to California. Cal-Fire provides excellent interactive datasets in CSV and GeoJSON formats.

Our goal is to determine which ecosystems are more fire-resistant by examining both fire-prone regions and severity patterns.

Modeling Fire Growth Over Time

These charts show the rapid growth in wildfire incidents and acreage burned in California.

California Seed Zones

California is divided into 85 seed zones based on climate and terrain. These determine plant adaptability and fire behavior.

This map highlights rare vegetation and eco-sections tracked by the California Department of Fish and Wildlife.

Fire Density by Seed Zone
